Kakara Pitha

Kakara Pitha is one of Odisha's most loved traditional sweets — golden, crispy fried cakes with a soft coconut-jaggery filling. Made during Nuakhai, Raja Parba and other festivals.

30
Minutes
8
Pieces
Easy
Level
Ingredients
  • 2 cups UTPAL Maida
  • 1 cup Grated coconut
  • ¾ cup Jaggery (grated)
  • ½ tsp Cardamom powder
  • Pinch Salt
  • As needed Water (for dough)
  • As needed Oil (for frying)
Steps
  • 1
    Mix grated coconut and jaggery in a pan on low flame. Stir until jaggery melts and mixture thickens. Add cardamom. Cool — this is your filling.
  • 2
    Make smooth, firm dough using UTPAL Maida, salt, and just enough water. Rest 10 minutes.
  • 3
    Divide dough into small balls. Flatten each into a small circle. Place a spoonful of filling in the centre.
  • 4
    Fold edges over the filling and seal. Gently flatten into a round shape.
  • 5
    Deep fry on medium flame until golden brown on both sides. Drain on paper. Serve warm.
UTPAL Tip: UTPAL Maida gives Kakara Pitha the perfect crisp texture. Knead the dough well for best results.

Soft Fulka Roti

Simple, soft, puffed rotis — a daily staple. UTPAL Aata stone-milled from Odisha's finest wheat gives these rotis a rich, wholesome flavour.

20
Minutes
6
Rotis
Easy
Level
Ingredients
  • 2 cups UTPAL Aata
  • ¾ cup Warm water
  • ½ tsp Salt
  • 1 tsp Oil (optional)
Steps
  • 1
    Add salt to UTPAL Aata. Slowly add warm water and knead into a soft, smooth dough. Rest under a damp cloth for 15 minutes.
  • 2
    Divide into 6 equal balls. Dust with dry aata and roll each into a thin, even circle.
  • 3
    Heat tawa on medium-high. Cook until small bubbles appear, flip, cook the other side briefly.
  • 4
    Move to direct flame — it will puff up. Apply ghee and serve hot.
UTPAL Tip: Resting the dough is important — it makes rotis softer and easier to roll. UTPAL Aata's natural texture makes a big difference.

Sooji Halwa

Rich, aromatic semolina halwa — made Odia style with generous ghee, cashews, and cardamom. Offered as prasad during Durga Puja and every celebration.

20
Minutes
4
Servings
Easy
Level
Ingredients
  • 1 cup UTPAL Sooji
  • ¾ cup Sugar
  • 3 cups Water
  • 4 tbsp Ghee
  • 10 Cashews
  • 1 tsp Cardamom powder
  • Few strands Saffron (optional)
Steps
  • 1
    Heat ghee in a heavy pan. Fry cashews until golden, remove and keep aside.
  • 2
    In same ghee, add UTPAL Sooji. Roast on low flame, stirring continuously, for 8–10 minutes until golden and fragrant.
  • 3
    Boil water and sugar together until sugar dissolves.
  • 4
    Carefully add hot sugar water to roasted sooji — stir quickly. Add cardamom and saffron.
  • 5
    Cook until halwa thickens and leaves sides of pan. Top with cashews. Serve warm.
UTPAL Tip: Roasting UTPAL Sooji slowly in ghee is the most important step — this is what gives the halwa its deep, nutty aroma.

Manda Pitha

Steamed sweet dumplings filled with coconut and jaggery — a treasured Odia recipe made during Raja Parba, Odisha's harvest festival.

45
Minutes
10
Pieces
Medium
Level
Ingredients
  • 1½ cups UTPAL Maida
  • 1 cup Grated coconut
  • ½ cup Jaggery
  • ½ tsp Cardamom powder
  • 1 tbsp Ghee
  • Pinch Salt
Steps
  • 1
    Cook coconut and jaggery together on low flame until thick. Add cardamom. Cool completely.
  • 2
    Mix UTPAL Maida with salt and ghee. Add warm water gradually to form a smooth, soft dough. Rest 10 minutes.
  • 3
    Flatten each dough ball into a circle. Place filling in centre, fold and seal edges into a half-moon shape.
  • 4
    Steam for 12–15 minutes until outer cover turns slightly translucent. Brush with ghee. Serve warm.
UTPAL Tip: Seal the edges firmly or filling will leak during steaming. UTPAL Maida's smooth texture makes it easy to work with.

Rava Upma

A quick, wholesome breakfast with UTPAL Sooji, mustard seeds, curry leaves, and vegetables. Nutritious, filling, ready in under 15 minutes.

15
Minutes
2
Servings
Easy
Level
Ingredients
  • 1 cup UTPAL Sooji
  • 2 cups Water
  • 1 Onion (chopped)
  • 1 Green chilli
  • ½ tsp Mustard seeds
  • 8–10 Curry leaves
  • 2 tbsp Oil or ghee
  • To taste Salt
Steps
  • 1
    Dry roast UTPAL Sooji on low flame for 3–4 minutes until lightly golden. Keep aside.
  • 2
    Heat oil. Add mustard seeds — let them splutter. Add curry leaves and green chilli.
  • 3
    Add onions and sauté until translucent. Add any vegetables and cook briefly.
  • 4
    Add water and salt. Bring to boil. Slowly add roasted sooji while stirring continuously.
  • 5
    Stir on low flame 2–3 minutes until upma comes together. Cover, rest 2 minutes. Serve hot.
UTPAL Tip: Roasting sooji first prevents lumps. UTPAL Sooji's even granule size ensures perfectly textured upma every time.
